About Us

Healthy sugar typically refers to a blend of about 95–97% sucrose with 3–5% L-arabinose. L-arabinose (also known as arabinose or pectose) is an aldopentose found widely in plants. Industrial L-arabinose is obtained from natural materials such as corn cob and bagasse through hydrolysis, separation, and purification.

  • Most sucrose may not be absorbed in the same way, which can help reduce unwanted effects associated with high sucrose intake.
  • Sucrose and arabinose that are not absorbed can behave like prebiotics in the large intestine, providing nutrients that support beneficial gut bacteria.

L-arabinose has a higher affinity for sucrase than sucrose (roughly 4–5×). Studies report that adding 3% L-arabinose to sucrose may inhibit around 60% of sucrose absorption and reduce the rise in blood glucose by about 50% under experimental conditions. Use AraBalance like ordinary sugar for meals, baking, and drinks—without redesigning your recipes.
